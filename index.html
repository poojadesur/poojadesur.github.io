Research

My academic work at the intersection of human-computer interaction and machine learning.

01
📄 CHI 2024 · Best Paper Honorable Mention

Calibrating Trust in AI-Assisted Decision Making: A Mixed-Methods Study of Expert and Novice Users

Pooja Desur, Alex Chen, Marcus Rivera, Jane Kim

We investigate how users calibrate trust in AI systems across two user groups — domain experts and novices — through a combination of longitudinal diary studies, controlled experiments, and qualitative interviews. Our findings reveal a significant "trust gap" between AI confidence scores and user mental models, and we propose a set of design guidelines for building more appropriately trusted AI tools.

📑 Paper 🎥 Talk 📦 Dataset
02
📄 FAccT 2024

Surfacing Algorithmic Accountability: How Explanations Shape Perceptions of Fairness in Automated Systems

Pooja Desur, Jane Kim

This paper examines how different styles of algorithmic explanation (contrastive, feature-importance, and example-based) influence users' perceptions of fairness in automated hiring systems. Through a between-subjects online study with 340 participants, we find that contrastive explanations lead to the highest perceived fairness, but can paradoxically reduce accountability — raising important design considerations for responsible AI deployment.

📑 Paper 💻 Code
03
📄 NeurIPS 2023 Workshop

Toward Participatory AI Design: Lessons from Co-Designing an AI-Powered Job Matching Tool with Underserved Communities

Pooja Desur, Marcus Rivera, Jane Kim

A position paper reflecting on a six-month participatory design engagement with a Chicago-based workforce development nonprofit. We document methodological challenges and lessons learned when co-designing AI systems with communities who are often subjects of, rather than participants in, the AI design process.
